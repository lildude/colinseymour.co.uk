For the buns:

450g strong white bread flour
150g of mixed dried fruit (raisins, sultanas, currants and candied peel)
7g dried activated yeast
3 tsp of mixed spice
1 tsp salt
1 rind of one orange (munch the fruit when you're done)
1 egg
200ml milk
100g of golden castor sugar
100g of unsalted butter

For the crosses:

75g flour
4 tsp caster sugar
4 tbsp water

For the glaze:

1 tbsp brown sugar,
2 tbsp milk,
1 tbsp marmalade

Method:

Dough:

Sift flour, salt and spices into a large bowl and mix in the yeast, fruit, rind and sugar.
Warm the butter and milk together in a saucepan until the butter has melted. Remove from the heat and whisk in the egg and add to the flour mix. Mix and knead dough on a floured surface for 10min until smooth and elastic.
Place into a greased bowl, cover (with cling film) and leave in a warm place to prove and double in size.

Split into 12 equal sized buns and place on a greased baking tray
Cover and leave in a warm place to rise for about 30 mins

Crosses:

Mix ingredients for form a thick paste. Transfer to freezer bag with the corner cut off or piping bag and pipe crosses onto the buns once they've risen.

Bake in pre-warmed oven at 200 degrees Celsius (400 degrees Fahrenheit) for about 20 minutes - they should be golden brown and a skewer should come out clean.

Glaze:

Mix ingredients and boil until a syrup forms.

Glaze your buns the moment they come out of the oven and then leave to cool.

Munch.

I made my crosses a little too runny and the hole just a little too big.
